Supervisors: Darren Gergle and Anne Marie Piper, Northwestern University

The aim of this research project is to design assistive technologies to better support creative expression of people with visual impairments. Gender Bias in Peer-Production Communities

Gender Bias in Peer-production Communities

Supervisors: Darren Gergle and Brent Hecht, Northwestern University
September 2017 - present

Peer-production communities like Wikipedia and OpenStreetMap have become important sources of freely available information nowadays. Despite having millions of contributors, these peer-production communities are alleged to have participation disparities, with men significantly outnumbering women. Focusing on OpenStreetMap, this project investigates the relationship between participation and content disparities to understand whether content of interest to men is better represented than content of interest to women.

Privacy-preserving and authentication in genomic databases

Privacy-preserving and authentication in genomic databases

Supervisor: Tanzima Hashem, Bangladesh University of Engineering and Technology
August 2014 - September 2017

We proposed a novel secret sharing approach to compute the probability of a patient to develop a specific disease without revealing sensitive data (patient's genome, name of the disease, disease marker of medical center, and the result of the query i.e. chances of the disease) to dishonest adversaries and ensuring authentication of the genomic data. We proved the practicality of our system via extensive experiments using real human genome datasets.

Outcome: COMPSAC 2018 , GHC 2015

Technology for Health and Well-being

Autism Support Smart Technologies For Children
Supervisors: Shamim Ahmed and Moushumi Sharmin, Western Washington University.
May 2017 - December 2017

In this project, I participated in a systematic literature review and qualitative coding of scholarly articles on smart technologies (wearables, smartphones, VR devices etc.) designed to support autistic children. We formulated a set of design implications to guide the development of autism support smart technologies.

Outcome: CHI 2018
Technology for Women's Physical and Mental Health
April 2016 - October 2017

We quantatively analyzed the impact of social networking on postpartum depression (PPD) in Bangladeshi Women. We conducted an online survey to learn about people's awareness about PPD in Bangladesh and proposed several design choices for tailoring social networking sites and apps to provide better support to mothers facing PPD. Apart from this, I co-designed a self-monitoring mobile app, PurpleAid, to handle common health hazards of women.

Outcome: ICCIT 2017, MediTec 2016
Health Support System with Biometric Data Acquisition Device
January 2015 - June 2016

We designed a novel health support system for low-to-moderate income people which almost accurately diagnoses diseases by measuring five vital signs of body with a biometric data acquisition device and taking symptoms as input from the users into a mobile application.
